Correlation Analysis

Understanding relationships between different environmental metrics

Correlation Heatmap

This heatmap shows the correlation between different environmental metrics. Values range from -1 to 1, where:

Positive Correlation (0 to 1)

As one variable increases, the other tends to increase

Negative Correlation (-1 to 0)

As one variable increases, the other tends to decrease

Key Insights:

  • Strong positive correlations (close to 1) indicate that the variables move together
  • Strong negative correlations (close to -1) indicate an inverse relationship
  • Values near 0 suggest little to no linear relationship

How to Interpret

The correlation matrix helps identify relationships between different environmental factors. For example:

  • If CO2 emissions and industrial activity show high positive correlation, it suggests that industrial areas contribute significantly to emissions
  • If forest cover shows negative correlation with emissions, it suggests that areas with more trees tend to have lower emissions
  • Neutral correlations (near 0) indicate that the variables don't have a strong linear relationship

These insights can help in understanding the complex relationships between different environmental factors and guide decision-making for emission reduction strategies.
