Feature Correlation Heatmap

This heatmap shows the correlation coefficients between different numerical features in the dataset. Darker colors indicate stronger correlations.

How to read this chart:

  • Values range from -1 to 1, where 1 means a perfect positive correlation, -1 means a perfect negative correlation, and 0 means no correlation.
  • Darker red colors indicate stronger positive correlations.
  • Darker blue colors indicate stronger negative correlations.
  • The diagonal is always 1 (perfect positive correlation of a variable with itself).
